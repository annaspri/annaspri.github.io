About Social Media TestDrive

Mission Statement

Social Media TestDrive empowers youth to become prosocial, productive members of the digitalworld through media literacy education and experiential learning in a protected yet realistic simulated social media environment.

About Social Media TestDrive

Social Media TestDrive is a platform created by researchers in the Cornell University Social Media Lab, in collaboration with Common Sense Education, the Program for Research in Youth Development and Engagement, and New York State 4-H educators. The project is generously supported by the Morgan Family Foundation. Like a driving simulator for young people learning to drive for the first time, TestDrive is a social media simulation designed to let students learn about and practice social media skills. TestDrive looks and feels like a real social media site, butall the other users on the site are “bots” that post content created by the research team. As a result, students can practice important social media skills in a safe and realistic environment without worrying about the consequences of making mistakes.

Each TestDrive module is designed to teach a specific social media skill, such as managing privacy settings, smart self-presentation, upstanding to cyberbullying, and information literacy. A TestDrive module consists of four parts. First, students will learn key terms, ideas, and behaviors related to the topic of the module. Afterwards, students get a chance to practice the behaviors in a guided setting. Then, students get to explore a simulated social media site wherethey are able to interact with the timeline, create their own posts, and get further practice in social media skills. The final activity in the module is a reflection page with questions about whatstudents did on the simulated social media site, as well as additional questions for reflection.

You can access TestDrive modules at the following link: http://app.socialmediatestdrive.org. For instructions on how to use TestDrive, please check out the “For Educators” and “For Parents” sections of our website. Please contact us if you have any questions!
